# Jacket Copy / Back Cover Ideas

Working notes for how to position the book externally.

---

## The Systems Thinking Frame

The book IS systems thinking applied to human conflict — but that language belongs on the jacket, not in the chapters. The book shows it; the jacket names it.

### Possible angles:

**The engineer's mind applied to human dynamics:**
> Logan King spent 15 years as a software engineer learning to debug complex systems — then turned that mind on human conflict. Most books about boundaries, consent, and harm tell you what to do. This one maps the invisible systems underneath: the feedback loops that turn fear into the thing you feared, the filters that make good people cause harm they can't see, and the leverage points where a small shift changes everything.

**The "why does this feel different" answer:**
> Every other book on this topic treats the symptoms. Be a better listener. Respect boundaries. Don't be a predator. This book maps the system that produces the behavior — so you can change the system instead of fighting the output.

**Short version:**
> A systems thinker's guide to human conflict. Why good people cause harm, why punishment doesn't fix it, and what does.

---

## Notes

- Don't label it "systems thinking" inside the book — that's telling, and the book already shows.
- The programming background works as a framing detail on the jacket or in an author bio, not in the introduction's credential list. The subject-matter credentials (Somatica, ISTA, Landmark, Tony Robbins, personal experience) carry the introduction. The engineering mind explains the *approach* — it belongs where you're explaining why this book is different, not where you're establishing trust on the subject matter.
- "Systems thinking applied to human conflict" is the positioning language for external use: marketing, conversations, interviews, pitches.
