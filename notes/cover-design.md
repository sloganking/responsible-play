# Cover Design Notes

## Color Palette
- **Background:** Deep midnight blue (dark, authoritative)
- **Text/Accent:** Gold — reflective/foil stamped in physical print (shiny, not matte)

## Front Cover
- Title: "The Wolf Who Cried Boy" in gold
- One visual element: a single abstract/geometric golden eye (not realistic, not Egyptian — clean, minimal)
- Subtitle placement TBD — test both with and without on front. If excluded from front, place on back.
- No wolf imagery. No mystical symbols. No busy design.
- The title does the selling. The cover lets it work.

## Subtitle
"Why the Most Dangerous Person in the Room Is the One Trying to Help"

## Spine
- Gold text on midnight blue
- Will stand out on a shelf among white/cream/bright spines
- Dark spine with gold lettering draws the eye by contrast

## Back Cover
- Subtitle (if not on front)
- 2-3 strongest one-liner quotes from the book
- Coaching link: sloganking.github.io/coaching
- Keep it sparse

## Design Principles
- Authority comes from restraint, not decoration
- Should feel like an industry standard — the definitive text
- Not self-help. Not academic. Not children's book. Not thriller.
- Professional enough for facilitators, evocative enough for the temple crowd
- A geometric/minimalist eye threads the needle between mystic and professional
- The visual confidence should match the book's voice: direct, unflinching, not trying to impress

## Production Notes
- Foil stamping for gold elements (reflective, shiny)
- Consider embossing for the eye or title
- Test both with-subtitle and without-subtitle front cover versions
