# Notice, Feel, Story

Three-step framework separating observation, emotion, and interpretation so you can verify before acting. Originated from Authentic Relating practices.

---

## The Three Steps

1. **Notice** — What a camera would have recorded. Pure observation, no interpretation. "His hand moved to my thigh."
2. **Feel** — Your emotional response. Actual feelings, not pseudo-feelings. "I feel uncomfortable." NOT "I feel like he's testing my boundaries" (that's a story).
3. **Story** — The meaning you made. "The story I'm telling myself is that he did this on purpose." Then check: is this story definitely true, or is it an assumption?

---

## Pseudo-Feelings

Statements that sound like feelings but are claims about others:
- "I feel abandoned" → "You abandoned me"
- "I feel manipulated" → "You're manipulating me"
- "I feel disrespected" → "You're disrespecting me"

These smuggle accusations inside feeling language. Replace with actual feelings: scared, sad, angry, confused.

---

## First Plausible Explanation Trap

The brain latches onto the first plausible explanation and stops looking for alternatives. NFS forces you past the first explanation by making the story explicit — visible as *a* story rather than *the* truth.

---

## Shortcuts

- **"It occurs to me as..."** — Flags your interpretation as an interpretation
- **"I imagine..."** — Acknowledges you're guessing, not knowing
