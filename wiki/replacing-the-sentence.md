# Replacing the Sentence

Finding the body's underlying story, writing its precise inverse, and speaking it out loud in a peak emotional state daily until the body believes the new version. Based on Tony Robbins' incantations.

---

## The Process

**Step 1: Find the sentence.** There's probably a single sentence — or cluster — your body has been operating on. Installed by experience, not chosen. When you find it, your body recognizes it: gut clench, tears, a feeling of "that's it."

Example (Logan's, running for 28 years):
> *"I am not safe. I can never stop fighting. I don't belong here. I am unwelcome. People reject me. There is no home."*

**Step 2: Write the opposite.** Not a vague affirmation. The *specific* inverse — one that addresses every face of the wound. If your sentence has six parts, the replacement needs six.

> *"I am safe. I can stop fighting. I belong here. I am welcome in every room I walk into. People receive me. I am home."*

**Step 3: Say it out loud, in state, with your whole body.** Get your physiology up first — run, walk fast, dance, music that makes you feel alive. Then say the words out loud, over and over. You're not reciting. You're retraining what your body suggests about the world.

**Step 4: Repeat.** Daily. The old sentence had years of installation. The new one needs reps. Same principle as everything else: the body learns from experience, not from being told.

---

## Your Primary Question

Your body also runs a **question** — asked constantly, without you realizing. The statement is what your body believes ("I am not safe"). The question is where it points your attention ("Am I safe?"). They feed each other.

Logan's old question: *How do I fix myself?* (Presupposes brokenness. No amount of fixing satisfies it.)
Logan's new question: *Do I like her?* (Presupposes value. Redirects attention to desire instead of fear.)

The replacement question shouldn't answer the old one differently. It should skip the entire axis — make the old presuppositions irrelevant.

---

## Why Understanding Isn't Enough

The old sentence got cemented during high emotion. Your body replays whatever meaning you made last time you felt that way. You can understand the pattern perfectly and your body still flinches when the phone buzzes. Intellectual insight ≠ body-level change.

The same mechanic works in reverse: peak *positive* emotional state → whatever you say to yourself sticks the same way the trauma did.
