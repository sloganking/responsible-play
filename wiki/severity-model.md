# Severity Model

The foundation for proportional response. Most conflict in these spaces is a fight over **how serious** something was.

---

## The Scale

| Level | Meaning | Examples |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **LOW** | Inconvenience, recoverable quickly | Awkward comment, minor social misstep, accidental touch quickly corrected |
| **MEDIUM** | Real impact, recoverable with effort | Boundary cross requiring conversation, emotional harm needing processing |
| **HIGH** | Permanent or near-permanent damage | Reputation destruction, assault, trauma that changes someone's life |

---

## Key Principles

- **Feelings ≠ objective severity.** Fear can inflate a LOW event into feeling HIGH. Fawning can deflate a HIGH event into feeling LOW.
- **Your reaction has its own severity.** Responding to a LOW mistake with HIGH-severity consequences (public labeling, reputation destruction) creates new harm.
- **Repetition upgrades severity** — a pattern of LOW violations becomes MEDIUM/HIGH. But only from your direct experience, not rumor.
- **Severity Shift**: everyday life skews LOW; play spaces skew MEDIUM by default. Mistakes have real consequences.

---

## The Self-Check

Before responding, ask: am I responding to what happened, or to what I fear might happen? The first is proportional. The second is the path to becoming a [[righteous-predator]].
