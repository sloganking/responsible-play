# Fawning

A trauma response where someone says "yes" when they mean "no." Not politeness — an automatic override so deep it can suppress even survival instinct.

---

## Depth

> "It's why one of my students didn't scream during her rape — 'I didn't want to embarrass him.'" — Kasia Urbaniak

Fawning lives on a spectrum from "said yes to avoid awkwardness" to overriding the body's most basic survival drive.

---

## The Two Victims Problem

When fawning happens, it creates TWO victims:
- **Person B** (the fawner) — had an experience they didn't want
- **Person A** (the other person) — was lied to, touched someone who didn't want it, may face accusations

The severity to Person A depends on what the fawner does after: if they own it → MEDIUM. If they cry victim and attack → HIGH (reputation destruction, witch hunts, permanent harm).

→ See [[two-victims-problem]]

---

## Responsibility

| Person | Responsibility |
|--------|---------------|
| Person A | Check in, read cues, create safety for honesty |
| Person B | Own the fawn, communicate after, don't blame A for believing you |

Even when you check in and they confirm yes — some fawners will say yes three times while fawning the whole time. Your final protection is your own felt sense.

---

## The Empowered Fawner's Practice

Announce your pattern upfront before play: "I have a pattern of fawning. If I say yes but my energy doesn't feel like a yes, check in with me. And I won't blame you for believing me."

---

## Healing Path

1. **Fawn** (automatic)
2. **Suffer consequences** — pain, resentment, suppressed anger
3. **Anger builds** until it breaks through suppression
4. **Set the boundary** — messy, late, but real. Immediate relief.
5. **Body remembers** — next time, the recoil from fawning overrides the fear
6. **Saying no becomes easier** — not from insight, but from visceral memory

The reverse bike metaphor: you're not starting from zero. You're overriding decades of training in the opposite direction. It takes longer than anyone who hasn't tried it would guess. But the pattern can be retrained.

---

## Where Fawning Ends Up (Without Breaking the Cycle)

Two endpoints if anger never finds an exit:
1. **Slow death** — constant low-grade stress that never boils over, metabolized as cortisol until the body can't sustain it
2. **Dark escalation** — rage, intrusive thoughts, [[sinsickness]] — the body's emergency broadcast when all other signals have been overridden for years
