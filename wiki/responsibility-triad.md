# Responsibility Triad

Three things people conflate that are actually different:

| Concept | Meaning | Direction |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Responsibility** | Seeing your causal part in an outcome (acts + omissions) | Forward — "how do I create differently?" |
| **Accountability** | Making repair for harm caused | Conditional — requires safety and good faith |
| **Punishment** | Inflicting suffering for wrongdoing | Backward — retribution for the past |

---

## Responsibility as Power

Popular "responsibility" = blame/punishment. Real responsibility = seeing your causal power and choosing differently. It's the tool that makes you a **Creator** (Drama Triangle shift), not a trapped Victim.

**Victim questions:** Why did this happen to me? Who did this to me? How do I make them pay?

**Creator questions:** How did my actions or inactions contribute to this outcome? What can I do differently?

---

## Everyone Has Some Responsibility

In any multi-party outcome, every participant has some responsibility — the boundary-crosser, the person crossed, bystanders, facilitators. This isn't blame distribution. It's power recognition.

---

## Key Frameworks

**Contributing vs Determining Factors:** Traffic contributes to being late. Leaving late determines it. When stating contributing factors, own your determining factor first — otherwise it sounds like "it wasn't my fault."

**Dice Principle:** You can't control the roll, but you can control the size of the die. Bad luck in a moment is real. Shrinking the die over time is your responsibility. (Logan King)

**Omniresponsible:** Your influence extends to everything you touch and everything you don't. A posture, not a burden.

**Responsibility Mirror:** Before pointing at others, look at your part.

---

## Responsibility ≠ Repair

You can be responsible for your part AND refuse to repair if conditions aren't safe. Repair-under-attack = [[fawning]]. Repair requires safety, good faith, and proportionality.
