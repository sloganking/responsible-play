# Over-Response & Under-Response

Match response severity to verified harm severity. Getting this wrong creates harm in both directions.

---

## Over-Response

Responding with higher severity than the harm warrants. Creates new harm. The most common inappropriate response in these spaces.

**How it happens:** Unqualified action (no verification) is automatic over-response. If you haven't verified what happened with primary sources, any disciplinary action beyond "none" is disproportionate.

**Named patterns:**
- **Scorched Earth** — Treating accidents like malice; destroying everything
- **Mob Judgment** — Group condemning without verification
- **Mercy Defense** — "I could have done worse" as framing for disproportionate harm
- **Weaponized Vulnerability** — Using victim status to justify extreme consequences

---

## Under-Response

Responding with lower severity than the harm warrants. Enables continued harm.

Often driven by:
- [[Fawning]] — fear of the consequences of standing up
- "They're just activated" dismissal of real threats
- Bystander passivity during someone else's over-response

For fawners: proportional response feels selfish. It isn't. Setting a boundary isn't over-responding — it's the appropriate response your body is telling you is too much.

---

## The Response Flowchart

1. **Pause** — Am I acting from fear or from assessment?
2. **Verify** — Am I a [[witch-hunt-dynamic|qualified source]]? Did I check with primary sources?
3. **Assess severity** — [[severity-model|LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH]]?
4. **Assess type** — [[mistake-matrix|Malicious/Harmful Belief/Unconscious]]?
5. **Check timeline** — Am I responding to NOW or to old trauma?
6. **Match** — Is my response proportional to the verified harm?
7. **Repair** — What does proportional repair look like?

---

## "But I Was Right"

Even if your assessment of the person turns out to be correct, responding without verification was still irresponsible. Judging decisions by outcomes instead of process is the gambler's fallacy applied to justice.
