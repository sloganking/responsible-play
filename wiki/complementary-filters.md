# Complementary Filters

When two people's [[filters-and-ras|filters]] attract each other like magnets and co-create the conflict both feared. Neither intended it. Their reticular activating systems were looking for something with polarity — and found each other.

---

## The Dynamic

**Person A** (The Scanner): RAS primed for predators. "They're everywhere. I need to find them."

**Person B** (The Target): RAS primed for attack. "People will misunderstand me and explode."

Person A scans for threats. Person B is anxious, apologetic, constantly flagging their own mistakes — which looks suspicious to someone hunting predators. Person A confronts. Person B fawns and explains — which looks like manipulation. Person A escalates. Person B apologizes for things they didn't do — which Person A sees as a confession.

The trap is total. If Person B fawns → looks like confession. If Person B fights back → looks like aggression. Both confirm the story. Calm boundary-setting has the best chance — but even that can be filtered as manipulation.

---

## The Five Patterns (Fear Creates What It Fears)

> **Person A (Scanner):** Hypervigilance creates false accusations. The hostility they create becomes "proof" there are predators.
>
> **Person B (Target):** Over-apologizing paints a target. The attacks they feared arrive because their behavior made them look guilty.
>
> **Person C (Cage):** Suppressing desire concentrates it. When it finally emerges, it's overwhelming — confirming the fear that it was "too much."
>
> **Person D (Fawner):** Can't say no → gets the boundary violations they feared.
>
> **Person E (Reverse Fawner):** Can't say yes → starves in front of a feast.

All five are the same mechanism: **fear creates a behavior that produces the outcome the fear predicted.** None of them can see it from the inside.
