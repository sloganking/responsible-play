# Mistake Matrix

Not all mistakes are the same. TYPE (intent/awareness) and [[severity-model|SEVERITY]] are independent axes. Getting the type wrong leads to disproportionate harm — often the responder causes more harm than the original mistake.

---

## The Matrix

| | Conscious | Unconscious |
|---|-----------|------------|
| **Intentional** | **Malicious** — Chose to cause harm knowing it was wrong. Rarest. Requires strongest response. | — |
| **Unintentional** | **Harmful Belief** — Genuinely believes they're right. Wrong, but not malicious. Needs education, not punishment. | **Accident** — Autopilot, impairment, momentary lapse. The Popcorn Metaphor. |

[[Fawning]] sits outside the matrix — the fawner's body acts against their conscious will.

---

## The Popcorn Metaphor

Unconscious mistakes are like burning popcorn. You weren't thinking. You weren't choosing to harm. You were on autopilot while the popcorn burned. You don't punish someone for burned popcorn — you help them set a timer.

---

## Why Unconscious Mistakes Happen

- **Autopilot** — Your body does something your conscious mind wouldn't choose (like the Iron Giant's defense programming)
- **Hunger** — Unmet needs (sexual, emotional, relational) drive behavior below conscious awareness
- **Impairment** — Sleeping meds, alcohol, exhaustion, emotional flooding

---

## The "Predator" Label Problem

Calling someone a predator collapses the entire matrix into one cell (malicious). It kills due process, dehumanizes, and prevents the person from being seen as human. If someone made an unconscious mistake and you call them a predator, **you're the one doing the predating** — causing HIGH-severity harm through a harmful belief.
