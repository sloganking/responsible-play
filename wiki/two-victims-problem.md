# Two Victims Problem

When [[fawning]] happens, it creates TWO victims — not one.

**Person B** (the fawner) is harmed by their own trauma response and had an experience they didn't want.

**Person A** (the other person) is harmed by being lied to, touching someone who didn't want it (without knowing), feeling icky about something done in good faith, and potentially facing accusations for believing a yes that sounded like a yes.

The severity to Person A depends entirely on what the fawner does afterward. If they own it → MEDIUM. If they cry victim and attack → HIGH.

> The fawner who cries Victim and blames Person A for "not catching their lie" is now creating HIGH severity harm to someone who was acting in good faith. The fawner who started as a victim becomes the perpetrator.
