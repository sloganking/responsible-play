# Righteous Predator

Someone who causes significant harm powered by moral certainty instead of selfishness.

---

## The Problem

We have a word for someone who causes harm for their own benefit — **selfish predator**. Everyone understands what that means.

We don't have a word for someone who causes equal or greater harm while genuinely believing they're saving people. So we call them a hero. Or a villain. Neither captures the reality: they're both at once. The **linguistic gap creates a perceptual gap** — we're pre-framed to look for selfish predators and miss the righteous ones doing ten times the damage.

---

## Why They're More Dangerous Than Selfish Predators

| Factor | Selfish Predator | Righteous Predator |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Scale | Limited — can't recruit ("let's be evil") | Civilizational — "join my righteous cause" |
| Self-correction | Knows they're wrong → might stop | Certain they're right → doubles down |
| Response to resistance | Often backs off | Escalates — resistance proves the threat |
| Duration | Ends when they stop | Propagates across generations |

A righteous predator has everything that makes harmful beliefs dangerous, plus:
- **Urgency** — "Something terrible will happen if I don't act NOW"
- **Moral certainty** — "I'm saving people, so extreme measures are justified"
- **Ability to organize** — "Join me in this righteous cause"

---

## The Five Dangers of Certainty

1. **Urgency bypasses verification** — no time to check if the belief is true
2. **Righteousness justifies escalation** — any action feels justified when you're "protecting"
3. **Certainty prevents self-correction** — evidence to the contrary gets filtered out
4. **Narrative recruits others** — "there's a predator and we must stop them" is compelling
5. **Resistance confirms the story** — fighting back "proves" they were right

---

## Historical Examples

- **The Inquisition** — Inquisitors viewed torture as "pious pastoral care." They felt righteous, not guilty.
- **Women's suffrage opposition** — Most men opposing it were Rescuers who genuinely believed society would collapse
- **Circumcision** — Started as a moral crusade to stop masturbation (Kellogg), became so normalized the belief is invisible
- **"Think of the Children"** — Four words that have justified more harm than any predator ever caused

---

## The Selfish Predator's Best Tool

A competent selfish predator doesn't use overt force. They see your blind spots and use them. They whisper the right things to a righteous predator, push the right buttons, and let the righteous predator do the rest. The righteous predator attacks with full moral certainty. The selfish predator gets what they want without being seen.

> Your blind spots are where the selfish predators hide.

---

## The Reformed Righteous Predator

A righteous predator who woke up, owns the harm they caused, and fights to be different. Has unique insight into the pattern from the inside — knows how the certainty feels, how the justification works, how the blindness operates. Valuable as staff because they can spot the pattern in others. See `i-made-a-mistake.md`.
