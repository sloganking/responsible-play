# Hiding the Wound

How concealing vulnerability guarantees the rejection it's designed to prevent. [[Fawning]] applied to your entire relational life.

---

## The Cycle

1. **Hide the wound** — Present the healed, put-together version
2. **Someone falls for that version** — They didn't consent to the wound
3. **Wound surfaces anyway** — Weeks or months in, under pressure
4. **They feel blindsided** — Mismatch between what was offered and what arrived
5. **Relationship buckles** — They pull away or leave
6. **Conclude: the wound is unlovable** — Hide it better next time
7. **Repeat**

The hiding creates the rejection. The wound always surfaces as a surprise — landing harder than if shown from the start.

---

## The "Fix Yourself First" Trap

"I need to fix myself before I can be loved" sounds wise. It's actually an infinite loop: you can't heal without love (some wounds specifically need someone seeing them and staying), and you won't let yourself be loved until you're healed.

---

## The Exit: Lead With the Wound

Show the wound first. Not as a trauma dump — as information. Same principle as announcing a [[fawning]] pattern before play.

> *"I have a wound in this area. This is what I need. This is what I'm like when it surfaces. I'm telling you what's real so you can decide if you want to be here for the real thing."*

Some people leave. Good — they would have left anyway. The ones who stay are staying for the actual you.

**Your wounds are advantages — if you let them be.** Carried with shame, they repel. Carried with ease, they invite. Same information, completely different energy.
