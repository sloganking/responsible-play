# Belief-Blindness

The inability to see past a belief so normalized it doesn't register as a belief at all. It just looks like reality.

---

## Mechanism

You weren't born thinking any of this was normal. You absorbed it from culture, parents, environment — until it became as natural and unconscious as breathing. The Inquisitor didn't feel weird about torture. Parents don't feel weird about helicopter parenting. People in a witch hunt don't feel weird about destroying someone's reputation. It all feels *obviously correct.* That's the trap.

Intelligence doesn't protect you from belief-blindness. It makes you better at *defending* the belief. People don't join cults because they're dumb — they drift into beliefs the same way you drifted into yours.

---

## How Language Creates It

Specialized words package acts so neatly you stop noticing what's underneath. "Circumcision" vs "cut part of the penis off" — same act, completely different feeling. "Heretic" let an entire civilization skip individual assessment. In these spaces, "predator" does the same: once someone has that label, due process feels unnecessary.

> When you notice a community using specific language that outsiders don't use, ask: if I stripped this word away, what would someone with no context call what's happening?

The gap between the specialized word and the plain description is where belief-blindness lives.

---

## The Self-Check

If you haven't been in the practice of examining what you believe and where those beliefs came from, you're navigating life with blind spots you don't know you have. The beliefs you've never examined are the ones running the most of your life.
