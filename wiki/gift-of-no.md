# Gift of No

When someone says no clearly, they protect both people. A clear no means their yes can be trusted. **Hearing no creates more safety than hearing yes.**

After enough experience with fawners who cry victim, a clear no becomes something to feel grateful for — not rejected by. The person who says no is taking care of you. They're preventing a situation that would harm you both.

> "Thank you for your no. Thank you for taking care of yourself."

**For fawners:** Your no is a gift to the other person. Telling someone no is a proper use of your power. Telling someone yes when you're actually a no is a misuse of your power — you're deceiving them and setting them up for consequences they didn't consent to.
