# The Promise

The facilitator's explicit public commitment that determines how incidents are handled. Sacred because it sets the container's integrity.

---

## What It Contains

- **The rules** — What's expected and what's not tolerated
- **The consequences** — What happens when rules are broken
- **The safety guarantee** — Good-faith mistakes will be treated as mistakes, not as evidence of predation
- **The protection** — Both parties in any incident will be treated with dignity

---

## Why It Matters

Without The Promise, the container has no spine. When something goes wrong, whoever yells loudest controls the narrative. With The Promise, the facilitator has a public commitment they can point to: "This is what we said. This is what we're doing."

The Promise protects [[top-and-bottom-vulnerability|top vulnerability]] as well as bottom — it explicitly commits to treating mistakes as mistakes, which means the person who made an honest error won't be destroyed by mob response.

---

## Key Principle

When the facilitator IS the one being attacked, The Promise still holds. Co-facilitators uphold it. The agreements don't bend because the target is staff.
