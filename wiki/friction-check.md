# Friction Check

Screens for **interpretive compatibility** — how aligned you are on handling ambiguity and conflict. Companion to [[rbdsmt]].

---

## Three Dimensions

**Story Style** — How someone interprets ambiguity. Do they default to curiosity ("I wonder what happened") or blame ("they did this on purpose")? Ask for a story about a past conflict and listen for the frame.

**Repair Style** — How someone handles friction. Conversation, silence, or accusation? When something went wrong in the past, did they seek repair or seek punishment?

**Communication Clarity** — Can they say what they mean directly? Or do they expect you to read their mind and get upset when you can't?

---

## Trust Baseline

Bidirectional trust that a mistake will be treated as a mistake. "If something goes sideways, are we both going to assume the best about each other and talk it through?" Without this baseline, even perfect RBDSMT conversations can't protect you.

---

## Don't Trust Self-Description

Ask for stories, not claims. How someone describes their past conflicts tells you more than how they describe themselves. Watch for asymmetry: if every ex is a villain, that's how they'll frame you.
