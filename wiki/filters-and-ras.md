# Filters & the Reticular Activating System

How your perception is shaped by stories you didn't choose — and why two people in the same room can have completely different experiences of reality.

---

## Core Model

```
EXPERIENCE  →  STORY  →  FEELINGS
```

Your feelings are caused by the **stories you tell yourself** about what happened — not by the event itself. The story happens so fast you don't notice it. Two people witness the same touch and one feels violated, the other feels compassion. Same experience, different stories, opposite emotions.

---

## The RAS (Reticular Activating System)

A brainstem network that filters what you notice based on what you're looking for. Everything else fades before reaching consciousness. You're not choosing to ignore things — you're literally blind to them.

**The Brown/Red Exercise:** Look for brown things for 10 seconds. Now — how many red things did you see? You can't remember, because your RAS wasn't tuned to red.

The RAS is what runs when you're *not* trying. Your default filter. And it doesn't just filter what you see — it filters what *options your mind considers*. If your RAS is tuned to threats, the thought "maybe I should look for friends" never crosses your mind.

---

## The Horror Movie Effect

After watching a horror film, every dark corner feels threatening. Nothing changed about your house. Your RAS changed. The same happens entering intimate spaces primed for danger — you'll find predators everywhere, even when none exist.

---

## Pre-Framing (Choose Your Lens)

What you consume before entering a situation shapes how you perceive it. Five hours of predator-hunting content before a play party = RAS set to predator detection mode. Every ambiguous touch becomes suspicious. You have [[responsibility-triad|responsibility]] for what you prime yourself with.

---

## When Evidence Can't Get Through

At high enough fear, even direct evidence gets filtered out. The Iron Giant's Kent Mansley: a boy is found alive and safe in the giant's hands, the general calls ceasefire — Kent screams "IT'S A TRICK! LAUNCH THE MISSILE!" and orders a nuclear strike on his own position rather than question the belief.

---

## The Predator That Was Never There

Imagine a room with no predators. Someone with trauma sees an ambiguous moment and sounds the alarm. Everyone's RAS activates. Someone fawns and apologizes (not because they did anything wrong — because they're afraid). The apology looks like a confession. Everyone goes home believing there was a predator.

No predator was ever present. But the trauma fed itself, proved itself, and grew.

---

## When Beliefs Cascade

The belief you were most certain about was load-bearing. When it breaks — through lived experience so intense the old prediction can't survive — the structure it supported becomes suspect. *If I was wrong about the thing I was most sure of, what else am I wrong about?*

A single transformative experience can change someone's entire trajectory across their life. The shattering cascades.

---

## Beliefs Aren't the Enemy

Belief as a **tool** is chosen and conscious. "I'm going to succeed no matter how long it takes." Belief as a **prison** is invisible and unchosen. "I'm unlovable." The goal isn't to stop believing — it's to see which beliefs are running you so you can keep the ones that serve and replace the ones that don't.
