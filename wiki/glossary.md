# Glossary

Complete alphabetical glossary of terms coined or specifically defined in *The Wolf Who Cried Boy*. Each entry links to its dedicated wiki page and identifies the source chapter.

---

## A

**Appropriate Response** — Matching your reaction's severity to the verified harm severity. Over-response creates new harm; under-response enables continued harm. → [[over-and-under-response]] | `appropriate-response.md`

## B

**Belief-Blindness** — The inability to see past a belief so normalized it doesn't register as a belief at all. Not stupidity — just invisible conditioning. Intelligence makes you better at defending a blind belief, not seeing through it. → [[belief-blindness]] | `why-rescuers-are-dangerous.md`

**Body Stories** — Trauma stored in the body as automatic meaning-making patterns below conscious awareness. Not memories you recall — patterns that fire before conscious thought. → [[body-stories]] | `body-stories.md`

## C

**Caged Desire Loop** — Person C pattern: suppressing desire concentrates it; when it finally emerges, it's overwhelming — confirming the fear that caused the suppression. The fix is smaller, more frequent expression. → [[fear-creates-what-it-fears]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

**Choose Your Lens** — You can prime your RAS for connection or danger before entering a space. What you consume beforehand shapes what you see. → [[filters-and-ras]] | `trauma-and-filters.md`

**Compass Stories** — Collecting stories, clips, and quotes that touch your heart as compass points toward who you actually want to be. Used to counter [[sinsickness]]. → `i-made-a-mistake.md`

**Complementary Filters** — Filters that attract each other like magnets. A predator-scanner and an attack-expecter will find each other and co-create the conflict both feared. → [[complementary-filters]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

**Conservation of Power** — Power can't be created or destroyed, only transformed. When someone "takes" one form of power from you, you gain another. Victimhood becomes impossible to sustain. → [[conservation-of-power]] | `all-power-is-mutual.md`

**Contributing vs Determining Factors** — Contributing factors are external conditions; determining factors are internal choices. Traffic contributes to being late; leaving late determines it. → [[responsibility-triad]] | `responsibility.md`

## D

**Dangerous Gap** — The period between when a threat is made and when it's acted on. This is when the facilitator must act. → `handling-threats-of-violence.md`

**Dice Principle** — You can't control the roll, but you can control the size of the die. Luck in the moment is real; shrinking the die over time is your responsibility. → [[responsibility-triad]] | `responsibility.md`

**Drama Triangle** — Stephen Karpman's model: Victim, Persecutor, Rescuer. The healthy alternatives (David Emerald): Creator, Challenger, Coach. The engine underneath the entire book. → [[drama-triangle]] | `drama-triangle.md`

## E

**Emergency Exception** — When imminent danger requires immediate action before full process. Not a loophole — a narrow condition for when someone is about to be physically harmed. → `when-things-go-wrong.md`

**Emotional Mirroring** — Matching someone's emotional state when calm doesn't reach them. A de-escalation technique of last resort. → `when-things-go-wrong.md`

**Explanation Trap** — When explaining yourself gets read as making excuses or manipulation. Common when someone is in [[narrative-lock]]. → [[witch-hunt-dynamic]] | `before-you-judge.md`

**Exposure Therapy for Fawning** — Practice saying no in safe containers until the nervous system learns a new pattern. The pattern breaks through experience, not insight. → [[fawning]] | `fawning.md`

## F

**Fawning** — Trauma response where someone says yes when they mean no. Not politeness — a deep automatic override that can suppress even survival instinct. → [[fawning]] | `fawning.md`

**Fawning Trap** — They said yes, so you can't know they meant no. The fawner's lie traps the other person in a dynamic they never consented to. → `before-you-judge.md`

**Fear Creates What It Fears** — The central pattern: the scanner's vigilance creates false accusations, the fawner's compliance creates violations, the cage's suppression creates overwhelm. → [[fear-creates-what-it-fears]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

**First Officer** — A trusted person who challenges the facilitator privately and supports them publicly. Based on the command structure principle. → `walking-your-talk.md`

**First Plausible Explanation Trap** — The brain latches onto the first plausible explanation and stops looking for alternatives. → [[notice-feel-story]] | `notice-feel-story.md`

**Framework Substitution** — Signal of [[narrative-lock]]: they stop talking about the specific event and start invoking a larger system (patriarchy, rape culture). They're no longer talking to you — they're talking to a category. → [[narrative-lock]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

**Friction Check** — Screens for interpretive compatibility before play: Story Style, Repair Style, Communication Clarity. → [[friction-check]] | `before-play.md`

## G

**Gift of No** — When someone says no, they protect both people. A clear no means their yes can be trusted. Hearing no creates more safety than hearing yes. → [[gift-of-no]] | `fawning.md`

**Good Thing** — An experience where everyone feels good both during AND after. The standard for play. → [[rbdsmt]] | `before-play.md`

**Gun Test** — Self-check: would you trust yourself with a gun right now? If not, should you be wielding intimacy at MEDIUM severity? → [[gun-test]] | `tools/gun-test.md`

## H

**Harmful Belief** — A conscious but mistaken belief that drives harmful action. Requires education, not punishment. Third type in the [[mistake-matrix]]. → [[mistake-matrix]] | `types-of-mistakes.md`

**Harmless Is Not Peaceful** — A person who can't be dangerous isn't choosing peace. True restraint requires having the capacity for destruction AND the wisdom to hold it. → [[harmless-is-not-peaceful]] | `harmless-is-not-peaceful.md`

**Hiding the Wound** — Concealing vulnerability to avoid rejection, which guarantees the rejection it was designed to prevent. The wound surfaces as a surprise, landing harder than if shown from the start. → [[hiding-the-wound]] | `healing-fawning.md`

**Horror Movie Effect** — Pre-framing your RAS to scan for threats so you find them everywhere, even when they don't exist. Like watching a horror film before walking through your own house. → [[filters-and-ras]] | `trauma-and-filters.md`

**Hundred Percent Control** — Both people have full power to stop or create the outcome. Not zero-sum — both hold 100% simultaneously. → [[hundred-percent-control]] | `tools/100-percent-control.md`

## I

**Implicit Memory** — A memory stored in your body that you don't experience as a memory. It shows up as an emotion — fear, dread, rage — that feels like a response to right now, but is coming from back then. → [[body-stories]] | `body-stories.md`

**Influence Firewall** — Tool for detecting whether someone's words install fear/urgency/guilt or observation/curiosity/verification. Consent for ideas, not just touch. → [[influence-firewall]] | `tools/influence-firewall.md`

**Interpretive Compatibility** — Alignment on how each person handles ambiguity and conflict. Assessed via the [[friction-check]]. → [[friction-check]] | `before-play.md`

## J

**Judgment Double-Edge** — The standard you set for others will eventually apply to you. The people who judge hardest collapse hardest when they make mistakes. → [[narrative-lock]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

## M

**Mercy Defense** — Framing disproportionate harm as restraint: "I could have done worse." Named failure mode of [[over-and-under-response]]. → `appropriate-response.md`

**Mistake Matrix** — 2×2 grid: Malicious (intentional + conscious), Harmful Belief (unintentional + conscious), Accident/Unconscious (unintentional + unconscious). Fawning sits outside the matrix. → [[mistake-matrix]] | `types-of-mistakes.md`

**Mob Judgment** — Group condemning without verification; acting on vibes instead of facts. → [[witch-hunt-dynamic]] | `appropriate-response.md`

**Motive Attribution** — Signal of [[narrative-lock]]: they tell you WHY you did what you did, skipping the question entirely. Your internal experience no longer matters. → [[narrative-lock]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

## N

**Narrative Lock** — When someone stops processing new information and filters everything to confirm their existing story. Three signals: motive attribution, framework substitution, no repair path. → [[narrative-lock]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

**Notice, Feel, Story** — Three-step framework separating observation (what a camera would record), emotion (what you feel), and interpretation (the story you made). → [[notice-feel-story]] | `tools/notice-feel-story.md`

## O

**Omniresponsible** — Your influence extends to everything you touch and everything you don't. A posture, not a burden. → [[responsibility-triad]] | `responsibility.md`

**Order of Operations** — For threats: (1) remove mistake-maker to safety, (2) contain violent person, (3) remove violent person, (4) address crowd. → `handling-threats-of-violence.md`

**Over-Response** — Responding with higher severity than the harm warrants. Creates new harm. The most common inappropriate response. → [[over-and-under-response]] | `appropriate-response.md`

## P

**Popcorn Metaphor** — Unconscious mistakes are like burning popcorn: you weren't thinking, not choosing to harm. You don't punish someone for burned popcorn. → [[mistake-matrix]] | `types-of-mistakes.md`

**Pre-Framing** — What happens before you enter a situation shapes how you perceive it. The lens you put on before walking through the door. → [[filters-and-ras]] | `trauma-and-filters.md`

**Primary Sources** — The people directly involved. Verify with them before judging. → [[witch-hunt-dynamic]] | `before-you-judge.md`

**Promise, The** — Facilitator's explicit public commitment: these are the rules, these are the consequences, and good-faith mistakes will be treated as mistakes. → [[the-promise]] | `before-you-facilitate.md`

**Pseudo-Feelings** — Statements that sound like feelings but are claims about others. "I feel abandoned" = "you abandoned me." → [[notice-feel-story]] | `tools/notice-feel-story.md`

## Q

**Qualified Source** — Someone who has verified with primary sources before acting. Unqualified sources + disciplinary action = automatic over-response. → [[witch-hunt-dynamic]] | `before-you-judge.md`

## R

**RBDSMT** — Pre-play conversation framework: Relationships, Boundaries, Desires, Sexual Health, Meaning & Mistakes, Trauma. Based on ISTA practices. → [[rbdsmt]] | `before-play.md`

**Real Repair vs Fake Resolution** — Real: both own their part and feel complete. Fake: one fawns or one takes zero responsibility. → `guiding-public-repair.md`

**Reformed Righteous Predator** — A [[righteous-predator]] who woke up, owns the harm they caused, and fights to be different. Has unique insight into the pattern from the inside. → `i-made-a-mistake.md`

**Replacing the Sentence** — Finding the body's underlying story, writing its precise inverse, and speaking it out loud in a peak emotional state daily until the body believes the new version. Based on Tony Robbins' incantations. → [[replacing-the-sentence]] | `invisible-patterns.md`

**Responsibility Mirror** — Before pointing at others, look at your part. → [[responsibility-triad]] | `responsibility.md`

**Responsibility Triad** — Responsibility (seeing your causal part) vs Accountability (making repair) vs Punishment (inflicting suffering). Most people conflate all three. → [[responsibility-triad]] | `responsibility.md`

**Reverse Fawning** — Saying no when you mean yes. Suppressing desire because expressing it feels dangerous. The mirror image of [[fawning]]. → [[reverse-fawning]] | `healing-fawning.md`

**Righteous Predator** — Someone who causes significant harm powered by moral certainty instead of selfishness. Has everything that makes harmful beliefs dangerous, plus urgency, moral certainty, and the ability to organize. → [[righteous-predator]] | `why-rescuers-are-dangerous.md`

## S

**Safest Place in the World** — Play spaces with facilitators, agreements, and 30 people ready to intervene on your behalf. The worst place for a selfish predator to operate — yet many still fawn. → [[fawning]] | `fawning.md`

**Scorched Earth** — Treating accidents like malice; disproportionate response that destroys everything. → [[over-and-under-response]] | `appropriate-response.md`

**Selfish Predator** — Someone who knowingly causes harm for their own benefit. Far rarer than [[righteous-predator|righteous predators]], and limited in scale because "let's be evil" doesn't recruit. → [[righteous-predator]] | `why-rescuers-are-dangerous.md`

**Severity Model** — LOW (inconvenience, recoverable quickly), MEDIUM (real impact, recoverable with effort), HIGH (permanent or near-permanent damage). → [[severity-model]] | `severity.md`

**Severity Shift** — In play spaces you cross into MEDIUM severity by default. Mistakes have real consequences. → [[severity-model]] | `severity.md`

**Sinsickness** — Moral immune system attacking you. Shame that attacks identity rather than behavior: "I AM bad" instead of "I DID something bad." Can be fatal to growth. → [[sinsickness]] | `i-made-a-mistake.md`

**Story Style** — Part of the [[friction-check]]: how someone interprets ambiguity — with curiosity or blame. → [[friction-check]] | `before-play.md`

## T

**Top and Bottom Vulnerability** — Bottom vulnerability (physical exposure) has a safeword and cultural protection. Top vulnerability (reputational exposure) has neither. The person who looks least vulnerable is usually most vulnerable. → [[top-and-bottom-vulnerability]] | `all-power-is-mutual.md`

**Trust Baseline** — Bidirectional trust that a mistake will be treated as a mistake. Part of the [[friction-check]]. → [[friction-check]] | `before-play.md`

**Two Victims Problem** — When fawning happens, both the fawner (who had an unwanted experience) and the other person (who was lied to about consent) are harmed. → [[two-victims-problem]] | `fawning.md`

## U

**Under-Response** — Responding with lower severity than the harm warrants. Enables continued harm. Often driven by fear or [[fawning]]. → [[over-and-under-response]] | `appropriate-response.md`

## V

**Victim Position Is Powerful** — Crying victim mobilizes rescuers, gains moral authority, can justify persecution. The [[drama-triangle]] Victim role isn't powerless — it's enormously powerful. → [[drama-triangle]] | `all-power-is-mutual.md`

**Vulnerability Flip** — After an incident, the physically helpless person often holds most power (moral authority, cultural sympathy, narrative control), while the person who had physical freedom faces the most catastrophic consequences. → [[vulnerability-flip]] | `all-power-is-mutual.md`

## W

**Witch Hunt Dynamic** — Accusation → fear activation → punishment without verification. The labels change (witch → heretic → communist → predator) but the pattern is identical. → [[witch-hunt-dynamic]] | `before-you-judge.md`
