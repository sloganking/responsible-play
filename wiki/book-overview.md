# Book Overview

*The Wolf Who Cried Boy* is a framework for seeing clearly — and for knowing what to do with that sight. Written by [[author-logan-king|Logan King]], it uses sex-positive spaces as a lens because that's where human patterns show up fastest and most undeniably. But every concept applies to life outside those spaces.

---

## Central Thesis

Most harm doesn't come from malice. It comes from people who can't see what they're doing — to themselves or to others. The rescuer who destroys what they love. The victim who doesn't see their own power. The community that mobilizes against an innocent person because the story *felt* true.

> **You don't fix blindness with punishment. You fix it with sight.**

## Writing Principles

The book follows strict writing rules documented in `notes/writing-guide.md`:

- **No moralizing language** — describe behaviors, not character
- **No defensive hedges** — don't say "this isn't victim-blaming"
- **Show, don't tell** — actions teach more than words
- **10x value mindset** — every addition must add significant value
- **Inarguable** — anyone on any side should be able to read without feeling attacked
- **The medium is the message** — the book practices what it preaches through its own language
