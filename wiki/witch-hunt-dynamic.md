# Witch Hunt Dynamic

The pattern: accusation → fear activation → punishment without verification. The labels change (witch → heretic → communist → predator) but the pattern is identical.

---

## The Pattern

1. Someone sounds the alarm (genuine fear or manufactured urgency)
2. Everyone's [[filters-and-ras|RAS]] shifts to threat detection
3. Ambiguous behaviors become "evidence"
4. Verification feels unnecessary — urgency overrides it
5. The accused's defense is read as manipulation
6. The accused's silence is read as guilt
7. The crowd converges on the scariest story
8. Consequences land before facts are established
9. Anyone questioning the narrative risks becoming the next target

---

## Qualified Source Checklist

Before acting on an accusation, ask:
- Have I talked to the **primary sources** (people directly involved)?
- Am I a **qualified source** (verified with primary sources before acting)?
- Is my information firsthand, or am I acting on secondhand accounts?
- **Witnessing isn't enough** — you saw what happened but your [[filters-and-ras|story]] about it may not be accurate

Unqualified source + disciplinary action = automatic [[over-and-under-response|over-response]].

---

## The Explanation Trap

When accused of an unconscious mistake, the accused often can't explain why they did it — because there was no "why." It was autopilot. The absence of explanation gets read as guilt, or their attempt to explain gets read as making excuses / manipulation. There's no good move — which is a signal of [[narrative-lock]].

---

## Historical Continuity

The Inquisition. Salem witch trials. McCarthyism. Each era thought the previous was barbaric. Each was certain *they* had the real threat identified. The pattern persists because it runs on [[filters-and-ras|fear]], not facts.
