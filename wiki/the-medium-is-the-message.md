# The Medium Is the Message

The book practices what it preaches. A book about seeing through stories must itself be free of narrative, moral judgment, and story — just accurate descriptions of behavior and patterns that are inarguable.

---

## How the Book Walks Its Talk

- **No moralizing language** — describes behaviors, not character. No "good person" / "bad person" labels.
- **No defensive hedges** — doesn't say "this isn't victim-blaming." Just says what it means.
- **Inarguable descriptions** — anyone on any side of an incident can read without feeling attacked.
- **The reader experiences clear sight** by reading the book, not just learns about it.

---

## Defended Terminology

**"Cry victim"** — Replaced "play victim" because "play" implied conscious deception. "Cry" is accurate (they're literally crying, proclaiming) and parallels the book's title.

**"Lie" (for fawning)** — When a fawner says yes and means no, the effect on the other person is being lied to. Softening this would be [[fawning]] to protect fawners from an accurate description.
