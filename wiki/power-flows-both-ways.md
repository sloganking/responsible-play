# Power Flows Both Ways

Every dynamic in the book demonstrates that power is mutual. The "bottom" of any hierarchy has weapons the "top" cannot wield.

---

## Examples Throughout the Book

| Assumed Power Holder | Assumed Powerless | Power the "Powerless" Actually Have |
|---------------------|-------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Men | Women | Accusations, cultural sympathy, reputation destruction |
| Facilitators | Participants | Witch hunts, hate mail, mob mobilization |
| The Accused | The Accuser | Moral authority, victim narrative, community protection |
| Public figures | Spouses | Lawsuits, public accusations, narrative control |

---

## Core Principle

> If you think power only flows one direction, you miss the weapons others have against you AND fail to take responsibility for your own power.
